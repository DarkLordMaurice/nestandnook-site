<h2 id="measure-first-check">Find your studs and check your cat's real jumping weight first</h2>

Locate wall studs with a stud finder before ordering anything — every shelf in this category is rated with the assumption of a stud-anchored install, and drywall anchors alone are a common failure point reviewers report repeatedly, especially once a cat starts jumping between shelves rather than just resting on one. Also check the shelf's weight rating against your cat's actual weight with real margin, since a jump briefly loads a shelf well past a cat's resting weight, not just its static weight on a scale.

## Modular system vs. simple shelf vs. budget set

The three products here represent three different approaches to the same idea. A **modular click-together system** (Feandrea) starts small and expands later, at a higher per-shelf cost. A **single decorative shelf** (FUKUMARU) prioritizes blending into the room over maximum capacity or expandability. A **budget multi-piece set** (RayCC) gets a full climbing path installed in one order, at the cost of a lower weight rating and a firmer stud-mounting requirement.

<h2 id="do-not-buy-if">Don't buy wall-mounted cat shelves if&#x2026;</h2>

- You haven't located wall studs, or your wall type (plaster, tile, exterior brick) can't take a stud-anchored mount safely.
- Your lease explicitly prohibits wall modifications and a full patch-and-repaint at move-out isn't something you're prepared to do.
- Your cat is significantly larger than a shelf's stated weight rating — check jumping-impact headroom, not just resting weight.
- You want a no-installation option — every shelf in this category needs real mounting hardware; there's no adhesive-only version that safely supports a cat's full jumping weight.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do wall-mounted cat shelves damage rental walls?**
Mounting hardware leaves screw holes, same as any wall-mounted shelf — the holes are small and typically fine to patch at move-out, but check your lease's policy on wall modifications first. A no-drill option isn't realistic for this category since the shelves need to support a cat's full weight and jumping impact, which adhesive-only mounts can't reliably do.

**How much weight do these shelves actually need to hold?**
More than your cat's static weight — factor in jumping impact, which briefly loads a shelf well beyond a cat's resting weight. That's why stud mounting (not drywall anchors alone) matters even for shelves rated for a cat's normal weight on paper.

**Can I mix shelves from different brands into one climbing path?**
Visually yes, but expandable systems like the Feandrea Clickat line are designed to click together only within their own product family — mixing brands means separate mounting hardware and no guaranteed spacing compatibility between pieces.
