<h2 id="measure-first-check">Measure your wall section before choosing a size</h2>

Find a clear, stud-backed wall section that doesn't compete with a workbench, door swing, or existing shelving — pegboard panels mount into studs, not just drywall, regardless of whether you choose metal or plastic. Measure the real width available, since most "standard" pegboard kits are sized closer to a 4-foot workshop wall than a narrow single-car-garage section. A 2-panel starter kit or a single 4-foot panel is enough for most small-garage tool collections; don't default to the largest bundled kit just because it includes more accessories.

<h2 id="do-not-buy-if">Skip a pegboard kit if&#x2026;</h2>

- You don't have accessible wall studs in a usable section — these mount into studs, the same requirement as the wall-shelving systems in our other roundup, not a drywall-anchor-only install.
- You're organizing a small handful of items — a couple of wall hooks may solve the problem without a full panel installation.
- You're renting and can't do any wall penetration — pegboard, like the wall-mounted shelving covered elsewhere on this hub, isn't a renter-friendly, no-drill category.

## How to choose

- **Size to your actual wall, not the "standard" kit size:** most pegboard kits online default to a size built for a full workshop wall — measure first, then pick the smallest kit that covers your real space.
- **Decide if you want accessories bundled or sourced separately:** Ultrawall, TORACK, and Pegboard Smith include a meaningful hook/bin assortment; a bare panel kit like Wall Control's starter set assumes you'll add more over time.
- **Metal generally beats plastic for load and durability in an unheated garage**, but plastic (WallPeg) avoids rust and is a lower-cost way to test the format first.
- **Confirm stud spacing before drilling** — a couple of buyers of the larger Ultrawall panel reported stud alignment issues; dry-fit against your actual studs before committing to mounting holes.

## Frequently asked questions

**How big of a pegboard do I actually need for a small garage?**
Smaller than most kits assume. A single 2x2 to 4x2-foot panel organizes a genuinely modest tool collection — measure your available stud-backed wall section before ordering, since the common "default" kit size on most listings is sized for a full workshop wall, not a partial one.

**Metal or plastic pegboard — does it matter for a garage?**
Metal panels generally hold more weight and hold up better to temperature swings and humidity, which matters in an unheated garage more than an indoor craft room. Plastic copolymer panels (like WallPeg's) won't rust, but are typically rated for lighter loads — fine for hand tools and light gear, less ideal for heavier items.

**Do pegboard kits come with everything I need, or do I need to buy hooks separately?**
It varies by kit — some (Ultrawall, TORACK, Pegboard Smith) bundle a meaningful hook and bin assortment, while others are closer to just the panel plus a minimal starter set. Check the included piece count against what you're actually planning to hang before assuming a kit is complete.
