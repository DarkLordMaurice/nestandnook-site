Let's start with the confession: for about a year, my "home office" was a dining chair, a laptop balanced on a stack of books, and a general policy of ignoring the ache in my lower back until 4pm every day. If your setup has a similar origin story, no judgment. Mine did too.

What finally changed things wasn't a dramatic overhaul — it was a handful of small, specific fixes, tackled one at a time. That's the whole philosophy of this site, honestly: **every nook wants a job to do**, and most of the time the job is smaller and cheaper than you'd think.

## The one that should've been obvious

My feet dangled. For a year. It sounds minor until you realize your legs are basically doing a very slow, very boring workout every single day, and your lower back is the one paying for it. A footrest sounds like the kind of thing you buy for your grandfather's study, not your desk — but it was the single fastest fix I made, and our team's actual testing backs that up. If you only fix one thing this month, [start with the footrest roundup](/home-office/best-footrest-for-short-people/) — it's the cheapest, fastest win on the whole list.
