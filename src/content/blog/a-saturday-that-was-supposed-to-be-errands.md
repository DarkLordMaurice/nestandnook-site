The plan for Saturday was three items long: coffee, a card for my neighbor's birthday, and a specific screw I needed for a shelf bracket that's been sitting half-installed on my wall since Tuesday. I want that on the record before I explain how the actual day went, because the gap between the plan and the day is, I think, the most honest thing I can tell you about how my brain works when it isn't pointed at a spreadsheet.

I stopped into a little plant-and-gift shop first, mostly to kill five minutes before the coffee place opened, and stood there longer than five minutes just reading the little care-instruction tags like they were a card catalog.

Coffee happened next, and so did a notebook, because I don't go anywhere without one. Twenty minutes turned into forty-five while I wrote down an idea for reorganizing this site's cluster pages, which is either dedication or a diagnosable condition — I've genuinely stopped trying to determine which.

## The record store was not on the list

Here's the thing about a used record store: you cannot walk past one and simply continue walking. This is a documented fact about me, not a metaphor. I went in to kill ten minutes and came out with a record I didn't know existed an hour earlier and a genuine debate with myself about whether I have shelf space for it, which, reader, I do not, and I bought it anyway.

<div class="polaroid">
  <img src="/winnie/winnie-life-record-store.jpg" alt="Winnie flipping through vinyl records in a record store" width="800" height="1000" />
</div>

The bookstore two doors down was worse, in the sense that it was better, in the sense that I left with four books and a mild sense of having been personally targeted by whoever arranges the staff-picks table. I have a library science degree. I am aware of exactly what a well-curated shelf is trying to do to me. I let it happen anyway.

## The dog was also not on the list

By the time I was actually running the real errands — bags in hand, list mostly forgotten in my pocket — there was a very good dog tied up outside a shop, patiently waiting for someone, and I want to be clear that this dog is not mine. I don't have a dog, much as this next photo might suggest a whole backstory I haven't earned. I crouched down, we had a moment, his person came out two minutes later, and that was the entire story. Some days the itinerary includes a stranger's very good dog and there's nothing to be done about it.

To be fair to myself: the birthday card did get bought, and dropped in the mail before the day was out, which felt like a genuine victory given everything else the afternoon had thrown at me.

<div class="polaroid tilt-right">
  <img src="/winnie/winnie-life-mailbox.jpg" alt="Winnie dropping a card in a mailbox outside a shop" width="800" height="1000" />
</div>

The screw is still not purchased. It's now Tuesday's problem again, which is fair, because Tuesday is the one who let it get this far in the first place.

If there's a point buried in all of this — and I try to have one, even on a day off — it's that the same instinct that makes me insufferable about drawer organization is the exact same instinct that makes me walk into a used record store "for ten minutes." It's not two different personalities. It's one very consistent person who occasionally loses a negotiation with herself, and I've made a kind of peace with that.

— Winnie
