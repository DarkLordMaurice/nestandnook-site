I have organized exactly zero Halloween parties in my adult life until this one, and hosting a Halloween party turns out to run on a completely different logic than hosting anything else — the goal isn't efficiency or a smooth-running evening, it's maximum theatrical commitment. Skeletons doing the wine pour. Candles everywhere they can plausibly not burn the house down. A soundtrack that makes the ice in everyone's glass rattle a little ominously. I went all in on the makeup and did not think through the practical consequences of that decision until much later in the night.

## The makeup was a whole production, and I'd do it again

The face paint took longer than getting genuinely ready for anything else I've done this year — a heart, a filigree line down each cheek, the works. I'll say plainly that the sugar-skull style borrows from a tradition I only really came to understand properly at a Día de Muertos parade later that same week, and looking back, I'd probably think a little harder about wearing it as a costume now that I know more. Worth reading if you haven't — it's a genuinely different thing from a party look, and the gap between the two is bigger than I appreciated going in.

## I made a new best friend and he did not have a pulse

  <img src="/winnie/winnie-life-halloween-skeleton-dance.jpg" alt="Winnie dancing with a costume prop skeleton at a Halloween party" width="1023" height="1537" />

At some point in the evening I ended up slow-dancing with a prop skeleton in a top hat, and I want it on the record that he was a considerably better dance partner than several humans I've danced with at actual weddings. No small talk. Impeccable posture, on account of having no choice in the matter. I have no further comment except that the photo exists now and cannot be undone.

## Apple bobbing is a young person's sport

<div class="polaroid inset">
  <img src="/winnie/winnie-life-halloween-apple-bobbing.jpg" alt="Winnie attempting to bite an apple during an apple-bobbing game at a Halloween party" width="1023" height="1537" />
</div>

I would like to formally retract every assumption I made walking up to that tub. Apple bobbing at eight years old is a game. Apple bobbing at fifty-plus, in full face paint, while a small crowd watches, is a genuine athletic event that I trained for exactly zero minutes and lost decisively. The apple won. I have never been more entertained by my own defeat.

## What I actually took from the night

None of it needed to be useful, and that was sort of the point — a night that exists purely to be a little ridiculous, with no organizing principle beyond "commit to the theme," turned out to be exactly the reset I didn't know I needed. Not every evening has to teach you something. Some of them just need a skeleton with better posture than you and a tub of apples that will absolutely humble you in front of witnesses.
